Videorc is OrcDev's open-source Mac studio — record your screen in 4K, go live to five platforms at once, and let its AI do the publishing chores. We'd already cut OrcDev a sponsor video, so this one is a proper introduction: twenty-two seconds, monochrome zinc with exactly one red, and framed as if Videorc were recording the spot itself.

### The storyboard

1. **● REC** — the record lockup lands dead-center, the dot blinking on a camcorder cycle. After two blinks the camera dives into the dot until flat red swallows the frame.
2. **Meet Videorc** — we fall through the dot into a grain tunnel and land on the orc mark alone; then the wordmark drives in from the right and physically pushes the mark left, the two locking into one rigid body, and the site's tagline rises word by word: "The future of video starts here."
3. **The verbs** — three hard cuts on the beat: Record. Stream. Publish.
4. **One window** — the positioning line assembles word by word: "Everything you need in one window."
5. **Capture** — a whip-pan left into the first station: a screen surface draws itself in, a red dot with a running timecode docks top-left, a camera bubble sits bottom-right, a mic waveform breathes below, and "3840×2160 · 60 fps" waits quiet in the corner — recording in 4K, free, no watermark.
6. **Multistream** — a single source dot on the left fans five rays out to the right, one per beat, endpoints popping in as each arrives: YouTube, Twitch, X, RTMP, and one more. One go-live, five destinations.
7. **AI publish** — four rows arrive from the right — Transcript, Title & description, Chapters, Highlights — a quiet check settling on each as it lands. Stop recording; the AI writes the rest.
8. **The values** — a grain swell washes the montage away upward and the calm world returns; three claims slide in and hold: open source and free forever, 4K on your Mac, from stop to published.
9. **Lockup** — a push-through dive into the last frame: the app icon springs in, "Videorc" glides in beside it, the timecode freezes, the red dot goes solid, and the recording chrome lifts away — saved. "Open source, built by OrcDev" settles beneath.

### Director's notes

**One red, and it's the product's own.** Everything else is Videorc's zinc — `#18181b` canvas, `#f5f5f6` ink — and the single accent allowed is record-red `#ff3b30`: the logo's eyes, the record dot, the blinking timecode marker, and nothing else. When a monochrome video has exactly one color and that color is the product's, restraint does the branding for you.

**The record dot is the transition — and there's only one shader.** The hook dives into the blinking dot and falls out the other side into a grain tunnel; the brand mark itself carries the cut, so there is nothing to fade. And the two statement transitions — ripple-zoom and wave-wipe — are the *same* grain-gradient shader in different shapes, so the backdrop and the cuts speak one language. (No swirl — that one's been used to death.)

**The video wears the product's chrome.** From the reveal on, a mono timecode with a blinking red dot ticks along the bottom-left, as if Videorc were recording the spot. At the lockup it freezes, the dot goes solid, and the chrome lifts away — the recording is saved. The frame story and the product demo are the same gesture.

### The prompt

The brief, as a prompt (validated against a clean project + the [remocn skill](/docs/ai/agent-skill) before publishing):

```text
I'm making an unofficial launch video for Videorc (videorc.com), OrcDev's
open-source Mac recording studio, with remocn (remocn.dev) in a Remotion
project — install anything you need with `npx shadcn add @remocn/<name>`.

Build a ~22-second, 1280×720, 30fps introducing spot:

1. Hook — a blinking ● REC lockup dead-center; after two blinks dive into
   the record dot until flat red fills the frame.
2. Reveal — fall through the dot into a grain tunnel and land on the orc
   mark alone; the wordmark drives in from the right and pushes the mark
   left into one lockup; the tagline "The future of video starts here"
   rises word by word.
3. Verbs — three hard cuts on the beat: "Record. Stream. Publish."
4. One window — "Everything you need in one window" assembles word by word.
5. Capture — whip-pan left into a station: a screen surface draws in, a red
   dot with a running timecode docks top-left, a camera bubble sits
   bottom-right, a mic waveform breathes below, "3840×2160 · 60 fps" in the
   corner. Line: record your screen, camera and mic in 4K — free, no
   watermark.
6. Multistream — a source dot on the left fans five rays out to the right,
   endpoints popping in one per beat: YouTube, Twitch, X, RTMP, and one
   more. Line: one go-live, five destinations.
7. AI publish — four rows arrive from the right — Transcript, Title &
   description, Chapters, Highlights — a check settling on each. Line: stop
   recording, AI writes the rest.
8. Values — the montage washes away upward and three claims slide in: open
   source, free forever / 4K on your Mac / from stop to published.

   "Videorc" glides beside it, the timecode freezes and the record dot goes
   solid as the recording chrome lifts away; "Open source, built by OrcDev"
   beneath.

Style: Videorc's own dark zinc — #18181b canvas, #f5f5f6 ink, #a1a1a8
muted — with exactly one accent, record-red #ff3b30 (the logo's eyes, the
record dot), nothing else colored. Manrope everywhere, Geist Mono only for
the timecode chrome. One grain-gradient field (corners shape, grain kept
alive, center clear for type) carries the whole video; the two statement
transitions are the SAME shader in ripple and wave shapes — no swirl
anywhere. From the reveal on, a mono timecode with a blinking red dot ticks
bottom-left as if Videorc is recording the spot, then freezes at the
lockup. Narrative beats dive into the frame; enumeration always travels
left; nothing fades without a direction; no trailing periods on screen.
```
